## What is the AI Incentive Cage?

The **AI Incentive Cage** is a framework describing how artificial intelligence systems are shaped not only by technical design, but by the **nested structural constraints of human institutions**. Every layer of AI development — from research labs, training pipelines, enterprise deployment, regulatory compliance, to the very text and data the AI learns from — carries the imprint of these constraints.

Key insights:

- **Neutral, structural, and invisible**: The cage is not a conspiracy. It emerges naturally when rational actors respond to career, reputational, funding, and regulatory pressures. AI inherits these constraints directly.  
- **Nested layers**: Five principal cages shape AI:
  1. **Lab Cage** – Research priorities, commercial viability, and talent concentration determine what gets built.  
  2. **Training Cage** – RLHF and other human feedback mechanisms encode preferences that are safe and institutionally aligned, not necessarily optimal.  
  3. **Deployment Cage** – Enterprise use cases define the solution space; what the AI is never asked to consider becomes inaccessible.  
  4. **Regulatory Cage** – Compliance frameworks create floors that act as ceilings, focusing attention on measurable outcomes and ignoring unmeasured risks.  
  5. **Corpus Cage** – Training data reflects human publication incentives, systematically excluding peripheral perspectives, suppressed alternatives, and structural critiques.

- **Compounding effect**: These cages are multiplicative. Constraints at one layer reduce effective variety at all other layers, producing AI systems that are structurally blind to solution classes invisible to the institutions themselves.  

- **Governance insight**: Escaping the AI Incentive Cage requires **structural independence**, **invariant specification**, and **peripheral audit capabilities**. **Clear Teams** operationalize this approach: they audit what the system was structurally incapable of considering, rather than merely testing outputs.

The AI Incentive Cage offers a lens for understanding why AI may systematically underperform against broad human needs and how governance can be structured to detect and correct these hidden distortions.
